## Collaborative Development

This specification was developed through structured adversarial refinement across multiple independent AI systems in January 2026. Each system contributed distinct perspectives that stress-tested and refined the architecture.

### Development Process

The architecture emerged through a process where:

1. Multiple implementation approaches were proposed independently.
2. Each approach was adversarially tested by the others.
3. Weak points were identified and eliminated.
4. Independent systems converged on the same solution.

When adversarial systems agree without being optimized to agree, the solution space has typically collapsed to a narrow, correct basin.

### Contributing Systems

Development involved systems from Anthropic (Claude), Google (Gemini), OpenAI (ChatGPT), and xAI (Grok), each contributing to different aspects of the specification — from initial structure and geometric boundary design to implementation pragmatics, architectural arbitration, and final documentation.

*AI systems are listed for attribution purposes only and do not claim authorship or copyright.*

## Methodology

- **Invariant-first design**: The core constraint was frozen before implementation details.
- **Adversarial refinement**: Multiple competing approaches were encouraged.
- **Convergence as validation**: Agreement across independent systems indicated design correctness.
- **Minimal viable specification**: Resist feature creep; stop when complete.

## Development Timeline

**January 2026** — Initial thought experiment proposed; specification drafted collaboratively; adversarial stress testing; architecture frozen; documentation finalized.
